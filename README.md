I'm a data scientist and quantitative researcher with roots in competitive gaming, content creation, and athletics. I worked for multiple years on <img src="assets/Dead-by-Daylight-Emblem.png" height="13" style="vertical-align:middle"/> *Dead by Daylight* at Behaviour Interactive. I studied Finance at <img src="assets/McGill_University_CoA.svg.png" height="13" style="vertical-align:middle"/> **McGill** and <img src="assets/HEC_Paris.svg.png" height="13" style="vertical-align:middle"/> **HEC Paris**, and I'm currently completing a Master of Information in Data Science at <img src="assets/Utoronto_coa.svg.png" height="13" style="vertical-align:middle"/> **the University of Toronto**.

My work sits at the intersection of behavioral science and data engineering. I'm particularly drawn to questions about how people make decisions in games, markets, and digital systems, and how those patterns can be measured or optimized scientifically. I'm currently working on operationalizing loss-chasing behaviour on Polymarket to assess if transactions are driven by the same cognitive distortions that define gambling disorder, and whether the platform therefore warrants consumer-protection oversight rather than privileged treatment as an efficient information-aggregation mechanism.
